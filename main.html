Amazon Why DSA

Amazon’s Vast Ecosystem

Amazon is not just an e-commerce platform. It spans a massive ecosystem that includes:

  • Amazon Web Services (AWS): Cloud computing powering startups to Fortune 500 companies.
  • Logistics: Managing how products are stored, delivered to customers, and transported using advanced systems and delivery centers.
  • Streaming: Amazon Prime Video, music, live sports.
  • AI & ML: Alexa, recommendation systems, dynamic pricing, fraud detection.

Why Algorithms & Data Structures Matter

At Amazon's scale, intelligent system design is essential. Here's why DSA is critical:

  • Efficiency: Millions of transactions processed in real-time.
  • Scalability: From startups to global operations, code must scale.
  • Optimization: Route planning, warehouse operations, and pricing models depend on optimized algorithms.
  • Low Latency: Instant recommendations, personalized search all rely on fast data access.

Project Objectives

This portfolio explains core Computer Science algorithms associated with Amazon’s real-world engineering challenges.

  1. Bridge Theory with Practice: Demonstrate how foundational algorithms apply to complex business scenarios across Amazon's diverse ecosystem — from logistics to cloud computing.
  2. Map Algorithms to Business Cases: Organize and visualize how specific data structures and algorithms solve Amazon-scale problems in delivery optimization, recommendations, dynamic pricing, and more.
  3. Showcase Impact: Highlight how algorithmic thinking improves performance, scalability, personalization, and drives innovation at Amazon.

Amazon Business Cases:

1 Subset Sum Problem: Optimize combinations of products in a shopping cart to meet specific promotional thresholds. (E-commerce Promotions)
2 Trie (Prefix Tree): Implement efficient autocomplete suggestions in Amazon's search bar, enhancing user experience. (Search Optimization)
3 Knuth-Morris-Pratt (KMP) Algorithm: Quickly recognize voice commands or repeated text patterns in Alexa's voice processing. (Alexa/Text Search)
4 Edit Distance (Levenshtein Distance): Correct typos in user search queries to return relevant product results. (Search Correction)
5 Longest Common Subsequence (LCS): Summarize customer reviews by identifying common sentiments across multiple reviews. (Review Summarization)
6 Johnson-Trotter Algorithm: Generate unique permutations for A/B testing different product display orders. (UI Testing)
7 0/1 Knapsack Problem: Select the most valuable set of items to ship within weight constraints in delivery drones. (Logistics Optimization)
8 Dijkstra's Algorithm: Compute the shortest delivery routes for drivers, optimizing delivery times. (Logistics/Mapping)
9 A* (A-Star) Algorithm: Estimate optimal delivery paths using heuristics like traffic conditions and delivery deadlines. (Real-time Navigation)
10 Bellman-Ford Algorithm: Handle dynamic changes in delivery routes, such as road closures or traffic jams. (Resilient Routing)
11 Floyd-Warshall Algorithm: Precompute all-pairs shortest paths between fulfillment centers for efficient logistics planning. (Route Preprocessing)
12 Ford-Fulkerson Algorithm: Maximize the flow of goods through Amazon's supply chain network. (Supply Chain Optimization)
13 Kruskal’s Algorithm: Design cost-effective network connections between data centers in AWS. (Network Design)
14 Boruvka’s Algorithm: Parallelize the process of connecting various warehouses in the logistics network. (Distributed Logistics)
15 Hungarian Algorithm (Assignment Problem): Assign delivery drivers to routes in a way that minimizes total delivery time. (Workforce Allocation)
16 Stable Marriage Problem: Match customers with products or services based on preferences and availability. (Recommendation Systems)
17 Min-Cost Max-Flow Algorithm: Optimize the transportation of goods to minimize cost while maximizing throughput. (Logistics Optimization)
18 Coin Change Algorithm: Determine the optimal way to provide change in Amazon Pay transactions. (Payment Systems)
19 Quick Sort Algorithm: Efficiently sort product listings based on various attributes like price or rating. (Frontend Sorting)
20 Tim Sort: Sort large datasets in AWS services, combining merge sort and insertion sort for efficiency. (Data Processing)
21 Segment Tree: Perform range queries on product ratings or sales data in real-time dashboards. (Data Analytics)
22 Fenwick Tree (Binary Indexed Tree): Maintain cumulative frequency tables for quick updates and queries in sales tracking. (Live Metrics)
23 Mo’s Algorithm: Process multiple range queries efficiently on large datasets, such as user activity logs. (Data Analysis)
24 Persistent Segment Tree: Access historical versions of data, useful in tracking changes over time in inventory. (Versioned Data Access)
25 Rabin-Karp Algorithm: Detect plagiarism or duplicate content in product descriptions across the platform. (Content Moderation)
26 Skip List: Implement efficient search operations in sorted data, such as user transaction histories. (Database Indexing)
27 Binary Search Tree (BST): Organize and retrieve product information quickly in the backend systems. (Data Retrieval)
28 KD Tree (k-dimensional Tree): Facilitate nearest neighbor searches in multi-dimensional data, like customer preferences. (Recommendation Engines)
29 Square Root Decomposition: Optimize query processing time on large datasets by dividing them into blocks. (Batch Processing)
30 Consistent Hashing: Distribute workloads evenly across servers in AWS, ensuring scalability and fault tolerance. (Load Balancing)
31 Bloom Filters: Quickly check if an item is in a set, such as verifying if a user has already seen a product. (Caching Systems)
32 Aho-Corasick Algorithm: Search for multiple keywords simultaneously in product reviews or descriptions. (Text Analysis)
33 Huffman Coding: Compress data efficiently for storage or transmission, such as in Prime Video streaming. (Data Compression)
34 Union-Find (Disjoint Set Union): Manage groups of connected components, like clustering similar products. (Recommendation Clustering)
35 Page Replacement Algorithms (LRU, LFU): Manage cache memory effectively in AWS services to optimize performance. (Memory Management)
36 Boyer-Moore Algorithm: Perform fast substring searches in large text bodies, such as scanning product descriptions. (Text Search)
37 PageRank Algorithm: Rank products or sellers based on importance and relevance, improving search results. (Search Ranking)
38 Conway’s Game of Life: Simulate dynamic systems, potentially modeling warehouse automation or traffic flow. (Simulation Modeling)
