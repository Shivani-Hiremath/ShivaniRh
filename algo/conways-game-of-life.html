  • Conwayâ€™s Game of Life is a zero-player game designed by British mathematician John Conway in 1970. It's a cellular automaton â€” a simulation where a grid of cells evolves over time based on a set of simple rules.
  • The game takes place on a grid (like a chessboard), where each cell is either Alive (1) or Dead (0).
  • Each cellâ€™s state in the next generation is determined by its eight neighbors (cells around it).

Rules of the Game:
  • Underpopulation: A live cell with fewer than 2 live neighbors dies.
  • Survival: A live cell with 2 or 3 live neighbors lives on.
  • Overpopulation: A live cell with more than 3 live neighbors dies.
  • Reproduction: A dead cell with exactly 3 live neighbors becomes a live cell.
