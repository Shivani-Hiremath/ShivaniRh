Skip List

Category: Data Structures, Probabilistic, Indexed Search

Description: A layered linked list data structure that allows fast search, insertion, and deletion operations — expected O(log n) time. It’s a simpler alternative to balanced trees with probabilistic balancing.

📦 Business Case (Where & How to Use):

  • Real-time indexing: Efficiently index large datasets for quick lookups in distributed databases and caching systems.
  • Dynamic ordered data: Ideal for systems where frequent insertions and deletions happen alongside fast search queries, such as messaging queues or leaderboard rankings.
  • Cloud services & distributed storage: Skip lists are used in distributed key-value stores like Apache Cassandra for maintaining sorted data efficiently with concurrency support.
  • Memory-efficient alternatives: When balanced trees are too complex or costly to maintain, skip lists provide a probabilistic, simpler data structure with comparable performance.
🧠 How It Works?
  • Multiple layers of linked lists where higher layers act as "express lanes" for faster traversal.
  • Elements are promoted to higher levels randomly, ensuring balanced height probabilistically.
  • Searches start from the top layer, skipping many elements, and drop down levels for precise positioning.
