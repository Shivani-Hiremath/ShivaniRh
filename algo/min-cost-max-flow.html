Min-Cost Max-Flow Algorithm

Category: Flow Algorithms, Network Optimization

Description: Computes the maximum flow from a source to a sink in a flow network such that the cost of the flow is minimized. It extends the max-flow problem by assigning costs to each edge and aiming to achieve the maximum flow at the lowest cost.

üì¶ Business Case (Amazon):

  • Assigning delivery trucks to delivery zones with minimal travel cost.
  • Mapping parcels to distribution hubs while minimizing fuel cost and delivery delays.
  • Allocating computing resources in AWS data centers efficiently based on load and energy costs.
  • Optimizing flow of goods in multi-tier supply chains with cost and capacity constraints.
  • Minimizing total shipping cost across cross-docking points in logistics networks.
üß† How It Works?
  • Construct a residual graph from the original flow network with capacities and costs.
  • Repeatedly find shortest path (with minimum cost) from source to sink using Dijkstra or Bellman-Ford with potentials.
  • Push as much flow as possible through the path found and update residual capacities and costs.
  • Repeat until no more augmenting paths are available that improve the cost.
